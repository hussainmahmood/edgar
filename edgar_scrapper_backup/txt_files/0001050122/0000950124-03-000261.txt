<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Item&nbsp;1. Security and Issuer.</B>
</FONT>

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The title of the class of equity securities to which this statement
relates is Common Stock, par value $0.01 per share (&#147;Common Stock&#148;), of 1-800
CONTACTS, INC., a Delaware corporation (&#147;1-800 Contacts&#148;). The address of
1-800 Contacts&#146; principal executive offices is 66 E. Wadsworth Park Drive, 3rd
Floor, Draper, Utah 84020.

</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Item&nbsp;2. Identity and Background.</B>
</FONT>

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This statement is being filed by David Katzman, Daniel Gilbert, Camelot
Ventures/CJ, L.L.C., a manager-managed Michigan limited liability company d/b/a
Lens 1st (&#147;Lens 1st&#148;), Camelot Ventures, L.L.C., a manager-managed Michigan
limited liability company (&#147;Camelot&#148;), and Lens Express LLC, a manager-managed
Michigan limited liability company (&#147;Lens Express&#148;), who are collectively
referred to in this Schedule as the &#147;Reporting Persons&#148; and individually as a
&#147;Reporting Person&#148;. David Katzman and Daniel Gilbert are the managers of Lens
1st, Camelot and Lens Express. Each of David Katzman&#146;s and Daniel Gilbert&#146;s
present principal occupation or employment is private investor. Lens 1st&#146;s and
Lens Express&#146; principal business was supplying contact lenses, solutions,
accessories and other vision care products directly to consumers and retailers,
until that business was sold to 1-800 Contacts on January&nbsp;30, 2003. Currently,
Lens 1st and Lens Express primarily hold for liquidation the assets and
liabilities of their businesses not transferred to 1-800 Contacts and the
proceeds of such sale. Camelot&#146;s principal business is being a private venture
capital firm. David Katzman&#146;s, Lens 1st&#146;s, Camelot&#146;s and Lens Express&#146;
principal business address is 100 Galleria Officentre, Suite&nbsp;419, Southfield,
Michigan 48034. Daniel Gilbert&#146;s principal business address is 20555 Victor
Parkway, Livonia, Michigan 48152.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;None of the Reporting Persons has, during the last five years, been
convicted in a criminal proceeding (excluding traffic violations or similar
misdemeanors). None of the Reporting Persons has, during the last five years,
been a party to a civil proceeding of a judicial or administrative body of
competent jurisdiction and as a result of such proceeding was or is subject to
a judgment, decree or final order enjoining future violations of, or
prohibiting or mandating activities subject to, federal or state securities
laws or finding any violation with respect to such laws.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;David Katzman and Daniel Gilbert are citizens of the United States of
America.

</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Item&nbsp;3. Source and Amount of Funds or Other Consideration.</B>
</FONT>

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pursuant to an Asset Purchase Agreement, dated as of January&nbsp;30, 2003,
among 1-800 Contacts, Lens 1st, Lens Express, David Katzman and Daniel Gilbert
(the &#147;Asset Purchase Agreement&#148;), 1-800 Contacts purchased from Lens 1st and
Lens Express specified assets in exchange for the assumption of specified
liabilities, the payment of cash and the issuance of 900,000 unregistered
shares of Common Stock. At the closing of the Asset Purchase Agreement on
January&nbsp;30, 2003, Lens 1st acquired 725,000 shares of Common Stock and Lens
Express acquired 175,000 shares of Common Stock pursuant to the Asset Purchase
Agreement. The closing sale price of the Common Stock on January&nbsp;30, 2003 was
$20.92. At that time and for

</FONT>

<P><FONT size="2">more than 60&nbsp;days before the Reporting Persons became the beneficial owners of
more than five percent of the outstanding Common Stock, Camelot owned 555,700
shares of Common Stock that it had acquired in the open market for an aggregate
of $6,602,174, using its working capital to fund the purchases.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Each of Camelot, Lens 1st and Lens Express had loans relating to its
business from a commercial bank, and Lens 1st and Lens Express acquired their
Common Stock as part of the purchase price for the assets they sold to 1-800
Contacts. Lens 1st&#146;s remaining $3,000,000 revolving line of credit, however,
is secured by the 725,000 shares of Common Stock it owns and the 175,000 shares
of Common Stock owned by Lens Express.

</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Item&nbsp;4. Purpose of Transaction.</B>
</FONT>

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lens 1st and Lens Express acquired their shares of Common Stock as part of
the purchase price for their assets sold to 1-800 Contacts pursuant to the
Asset Purchase Agreement, as described in Item&nbsp;3. Camelot acquired the shares
of Common Stock beneficially owned by it for investment purposes.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In connection with the Asset Purchase Agreement, 1-800 Contacts, Jonathan
Coon, Stephen A. Yacktman, Jason Subotky and John F. Nichols (the &#147;Principal
Stockholders&#148;), Lens 1st, Lens Express and David Katzman entered into a Voting
Agreement, dated as of January&nbsp;30, 2003 (the &#147;Voting Agreement&#148;). Pursuant to
the Voting Agreement, the size of 1-800 Contacts&#146; Board of Directors was
increased, and David Katzman was elected as a Class&nbsp;II Director of 1-800
Contacts on January&nbsp;31, 2003. In addition, 1-800 Contacts has agreed to
nominate and recommend David Katzman for election as a Class&nbsp;II Director at the
2003 Annual Meeting of Stockholders of 1-800 Contacts for a term expiring at
the 2006 Annual Meeting of Stockholders, and the Principal Stockholders, Lens
1st and Lens Express have agreed to vote for his election, subject to the terms
and conditions of the Voting Agreement. The Reporting Persons disclaim
beneficial ownership of the Common Stock owned by the Principal Stockholders.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Also in connection with the Asset Purchase Agreement, 1-800 Contacts, Lens
1st, Lens Express, David Katzman and Daniel Gilbert entered into a Lock-Up
Agreement, dated as of January&nbsp;30, 2003 (the &#147;Lock-Up Agreement&#148;). Pursuant to
the Lock-Up Agreement, Lens 1st, Lens Express, David Katzman and Daniel Gilbert
(collectively, the &#147;Shareholders&#148;) agreed not to sell or otherwise dispose of,
directly or indirectly, the 900,000 shares of Common Stock issued pursuant to
the Asset Purchase Agreement for 12&nbsp;months following January&nbsp;30, 2003, subject
to various exceptions described in the Lock-Up Agreement that generally either
(1)&nbsp;do not involve open market transactions without 1-800 Contacts&#146; consent or
(2)&nbsp;involve business combinations.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Also in connection with the Asset Purchase Agreement, 1-800 Contacts, Lens
1st and Lens Express entered into a Registration Rights Agreement, dated as of
January&nbsp;30, 2003 (the &#147;Registration Rights Agreement&#148;). The Registration
Rights Agreement generally provides Lens 1st, Lens Express and specified
assignees with piggyback registration rights with respect to 900,000 shares of
Common Stock.

</FONT>

of Common Stock in the open market, in privately negotiated transactions, by

Stock or not to dispose of all or part of such Common Stock if he or it

that time.

<P align="right"><FONT size="2"><B>EXHIBIT 99.2</B></FONT>

<P align="center"><FONT size="2"><B>VOTING AGREEMENT</B>
</FONT>




<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THIS VOTING AGREEMENT (the &#147;Agreement&#148;) is made as of the 30th day of
January, 2003 by and among 1-800 CONTACTS, INC., a Delaware corporation (the
&#147;Company&#148;), and Jonathan C. Coon, Stephen A. Yacktman, Jason S. Subotky and
John F. Nichols (the &#147;Principal Stockholders&#148;), and CAMELOT VENTURES/CJ, L.L.C.
d/b/a LENS 1st, a Michigan limited liability company, and LENS EXPRESS LLC, a
Michigan limited liability company (the &#147;Investors&#148;), and David Katzman (&#147;Mr.
Katzman&#148;).

</FONT>


<!-- link1 "RECITALS" -->
<P align="center"><FONT size="2"><B>RECITALS</B>
</FONT>

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company, the Investors and Mr.&nbsp;Katzman have entered into an Asset
Purchase Agreement (the &#147;Purchase Agreement&#148;) of even date herewith pursuant to
which the Company is delivering to the Investors, among other things, nine
hundred thousand (900,000) shares of the Company&#146;s $0.01 par value common stock
(the &#147;Closing Buyer Shares&#148;). A requirement under the Purchase Agreement is
that the Company, the Principal Stockholders, the Investors and Mr.&nbsp;Katzman
enter into this Agreement for the purpose of setting forth the terms and
conditions pursuant to which the Principal Stockholders shall vote their shares
of the Company&#146;s voting stock in favor of electing, and against removing, the
Investors&#146; designee, Mr.&nbsp;Katzman, as a member of the Company&#146;s Board of
Directors in accordance with the Company&#146;s certificate of incorporation and
bylaws, as amended. The Company, the Investors, the Principal Stockholders and
Mr.&nbsp;Katzman each desire to facilitate the voting arrangements set forth in this
Agreement by agreeing to the terms and conditions set forth below. All terms
not defined herein shall have the meaning ascribed to such terms in the
Purchase Agreement.

</FONT>


<!-- link1 "AGREEMENT" -->
<P align="center"><FONT size="2"><B>AGREEMENT</B>
</FONT>

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The parties agree as follows:

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Election of Directors.</B>

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1.1 Board Representation. At the first meeting of the Board of Directors
of the Company after the delivery of the Closing Buyer Shares, currently
contemplated for January&nbsp;31, 2003, the Company&#146;s Board of Directors shall
increase the number of Directors which shall constitute the Company&#146;s Board of
Directors and the resulting vacancy shall be filled by the Company&#146;s Board of
Directors electing Mr.&nbsp;Katzman as a Class&nbsp;II Director, with a term expiring at
the 2003 Annual Meeting of Stockholders. The Company and its Board of
Directors shall nominate and recommend Mr.&nbsp;Katzman for election as a Class&nbsp;II
Director of the Company at the 2003 Annual Meeting of Stockholders for a term
expiring at the 2006 Annual Meeting of Stockholders. At the 2003 Annual
Meeting of Stockholders, the Principal Stockholders and the Investors agree to
vote or act with respect to their shares of the Company&#146;s common stock so as to
elect the Investors&#146; designee, currently Mr.&nbsp;Katzman, as a Class&nbsp;II member of
the Company&#146;s Board of Directors for a term expiring at the 2006 Annual Meeting
of Stockholders, unless this Agreement is terminated before the 2003 Annual
Meeting of Stockholders pursuant to Section&nbsp;2

</FONT>


<P align="center"><FONT size="2">&nbsp;</FONT>

<!-- PAGEBREAK -->
<P><HR noshade><P>
<H5 align="left" style="page-break-before:always">&nbsp;</H5><P>
<P><FONT size="2">below; provided, however, that with respect to the foregoing sentence, if
the Investors designate a person to serve as director other than Mr.&nbsp;Katzman,
then the person so designated shall be subject to the reasonable approval of a
majority of the directors of the Company then serving in such capacity, which
directors shall not include the director or directors of the Company who is, or
was, serving as the previous designee of the Investors on the Company&#146;s Board
of Directors. At any other meeting or action by written consent of the
Company&#146;s stockholders during the term of this Agreement at or by which the
Investors&#146; designee is proposed to be removed as a member of the Company&#146;s
Board of Directors, the Principal Stockholders and the Investors shall vote or
act with respect to their shares of the Company&#146;s common stock so as to oppose
the removal of the Investors&#146; designee as a member of the Company&#146;s Board of
Directors, unless there is cause for such removal pursuant to Section&nbsp;1.3.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1.2 Appointment of Directors. In the event of the resignation or death,
removal or disqualification of a director selected pursuant to Section&nbsp;1.1
above, the Investors shall promptly nominate a new director, and, after written
notice of the nomination has been given by the Investors to the other parties
(and, with respect to a nominee designated by the Investors, other than Mr.
Katzman, such nominee has been approved by the Company&#146;s directors as provided
in Section&nbsp;1.1 above), each of the Investors and the Principal Stockholders
shall vote its shares of capital stock of the Company to elect such nominee to
the Board of Directors.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1.3 Removal. The Investors may change their designated director at any
time and from time to time, with or without cause (subject to the Certificate
of Incorporation and Bylaws of the Company as in effect from time to time and
any requirements of law), in their sole discretion, and after written notice to
each of the parties hereto of the new nominee to replace such director (and
with respect to a nominee designated by the Investors, other than Mr.&nbsp;Katzman,
after such nominee has been approved by the Company&#146;s directors in accordance
with Section&nbsp;1.1 above), each of the Investors and the Principal Stockholders
shall promptly vote its shares of capital stock of the Company to remove the
former designee from, and to elect such nominee to, the Board of Directors.
Notwithstanding the foregoing, however, the Company may remove the Investors&#146;
designee to the Board of Directors of the Company, in the event such designee
(a)&nbsp;is or becomes subject to an enforcement action, or is or becomes the only
member of the Company&#146;s Board of Directors subject to a publicly announced
investigation, brought by the U.S. Securities &#038; Exchange Commission or the
NASD, or (b)&nbsp;is or becomes subject to a criminal investigation or other
proceeding in which criminal activity is alleged. For purposes of this Section
1.3, the Company shall not be entitled to remove the Investor&#146;s designee to the
Board of Directors of the Company merely because such nominee does not
constitute an &#147;independent&#148; director, as defined by the rules and regulations
of The Nasdaq Stock Market, Inc.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1.4 No Revocation. Except as expressly provided herein, the voting
agreements contained herein are coupled with an interest and may not be revoked
during the term of this Agreement.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1.5 Successors to the Investors. If the Investors distribute the Closing
Buyer Shares to their members, the holder of the largest number of Closing
Buyer Shares, as identified in writing by the Investors, shall succeed to the
Investors&#146; rights and obligations under this Agreement upon agreeing in writing
to be bound by the terms of this Agreement.

</FONT>

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Termination.</B>

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;2.1 Termination Events. This Agreement shall terminate upon the earlier
of:

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a)&nbsp;January&nbsp;30, 2006;

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b)&nbsp;Investors, their members and Affiliates of the foregoing holding, in
the aggregate, less than five percent (5%) of the issued and outstanding common
stock of the Company; or

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c)&nbsp;The sale, conveyance or disposal of all or substantially all of the
Company&#146;s property or business to a third party not directly or indirectly
owned at least 50% by the Company&#146;s stockholders before such sale, conveyance
or disposal or the Company&#146;s merger with or into or consolidation with any
other corporation (other than a wholly-owned subsidiary corporation) after
which the Company&#146;s stockholders immediately before such merger or
consolidation no longer own, directly or indirectly, at least 50% of the
surviving entity, or if the Company effects any other transaction or series of
related transactions in which more than fifty percent (50%) of the voting power
of the Company is disposed of, provided that this Section&nbsp;2.1(b) shall not
apply to a merger effected exclusively for the purpose of changing the domicile
of the Company.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Miscellaneous.</B>

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;3.1 Successors and Assigns. The terms and conditions of this Agreement
shall inure to the benefit of and be binding upon the respective successors and
assigns of the parties. Nothing in this Agreement, express or implied, is
intended to confer upon any party other than the parties hereto or their
respective successors and assigns any rights, remedies, obligations, or
liabilities under or by reason of this Agreement, except as expressly provided
in this Agreement.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;3.2 Amendments and Waivers. Any term hereof may be amended or waived only
with the written consent of the Company, the Investors, the Principal
Stockholders and Mr.&nbsp;Katzman. Any amendment or waiver effected in accordance
with this Section&nbsp;3.2 shall be binding upon the Company, the Investors and the
Principal Stockholders and each of their respective successors and assigns.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;3.3 Notices. Any notice required or permitted by this Agreement shall be
in writing and shall be deemed sufficient on the date of delivery, when
delivered personally or by overnight courier or sent by telegram or fax, or
seventy-two (72)&nbsp;hours after being deposited in the U.S. mail, as certified or
registered mail, with postage prepaid, and addressed to the party to be
notified at such party&#146;s address or fax number as set forth on the signature
page hereto, or as subsequently modified by written notice.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;3.4 Severability. If one or more provisions of this Agreement are held to
be unenforceable under applicable law, the parties agree to renegotiate such
provision in good faith. In the event that the parties cannot reach a mutually
agreeable and enforceable replacement for such provision, then (a)&nbsp;such
provision shall be excluded from this Agreement, (b)&nbsp;the balance of

</FONT>

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;3.5 Governing Law and Jurisdiction. This Agreement and all acts and
transactions pursuant hereto and the rights and obligations of the parties
hereto shall be governed, construed and interpreted in accordance with the laws
of the State of Utah without giving effect to principles of conflicts of law.
The jurisdiction and venue of any legal action under this Agreement shall be as
set forth in the Purchase Agreement.

which together shall constitute one instrument.

<P align="right"><FONT size="2"><B>EXHIBIT 99.3</B></FONT>

<P align="center"><FONT size="2"><B>LOCK-UP AGREEMENT</B>
</FONT>




<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THIS LOCK-UP AGREEMENT (this &#147;Agreement&#148;), is made and entered into as of
January&nbsp;30, 2003 (the &#147;Effective Date&#148;) by and among 1-800 CONTACTS, INC., a
Delaware corporation (the &#147;Company&#148;), CAMELOT VENTURES/CJ, L.L.C. d/b/a LENS
1st, a Michigan limited liability company (&#147;Lens 1st&#148;), LENS EXPRESS LLC, a
Michigan limited liability company (&#147;Lens Express&#148;), Dan Gilbert, an individual
(&#147;Gilbert&#148;), and David Katzman, an individual (&#147;Katzman&#148;). Lens 1st, Lens
Express, Gilbert and Katzman are collectively referred to herein as the
&#147;Shareholders.&#148;

</FONT>


<P align="center"><FONT size="2"><B>RECITALS</B>
</FONT>




<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lens 1st, Lens Express, Gilbert, Katzman and the Company have entered
into an Asset Purchase Agreement, dated January&nbsp;30, 2003 (the &#147;Purchase
Agreement&#148;), pursuant to which the Company is purchasing certain of the assets
and is assuming certain of the liabilities of Lens 1st and Lens Express (the
&#147;Transaction&#148;).

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As partial consideration for the Transaction, the Company will issue
900,000 shares of its $0.01 par value common stock (the &#147;Common Stock&#148;) to Lens
1st and Lens Express (the &#147;Shares&#148;).

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a condition to the closing of the Transaction, each of the
Shareholders is required to enter into a lock-up agreement covering the Shares
(collectively, the &#147;Lock-up Shares&#148;).

</FONT>


<P align="center"><FONT size="2"><B>AGREEMENT</B>
</FONT>




<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The parties hereby agree as follows:

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lock-up</B>. The Shareholders agree that they will not offer, sell or
contract to sell, trade, transfer or otherwise dispose of, directly or
indirectly (including short sales, sales against the box and/or other hedging
or derivative transactions) (collectively, &#147;Transfer&#148;), the Lock-Up Shares for
a period of twelve (12)&nbsp;months following the Effective Date (the &#147;Lock-Up
Period&#148;), except (1)&nbsp;Transfers with the Company&#146;s prior written consent , (2)
Transfers by Lens 1st and/or Lens Express to any of their members (&#147;Members&#148;)
who agree in writing to be bound by the terms of this Agreement as a
Shareholder as provided in Section&nbsp;4 below, (3)&nbsp;Transfers to a Member&#146;s
immediate family or blood relative (&#147;Relative&#148;) who agree to act through a
single representative who agrees in writing to be bound by the terms of this
Agreement as a Shareholder as provided in Section&nbsp;4 below, (4)&nbsp;Transfers to any
trust which has as its only beneficiaries one or more Relatives, to the extent
any such beneficiaries agree in writing to be bound by the terms of this
Agreement as a Shareholder as provided in Section&nbsp;4 below, (5)&nbsp;Transfers to any
entity controlled by one or more Members which agrees in writing to be bound by
the terms of this Agreement as a Shareholder as provided in Section&nbsp;4 below,
(6)&nbsp;Transfers by Members, upon death, by will, pursuant to the applicable laws
of descent and distribution, by other applicable law, pursuant to the terms of
the applicable trust, or by any combination of the foregoing, if the transferee
agrees in writing to be

</FONT>

Section&nbsp;4 below, and (7)&nbsp;Transfers by bonafide gift if the transferee agrees in
writing to be bound by the terms of this Agreement as a Shareholder as provided
in Section&nbsp;4 below. In the event that any of the Shareholders breaches the
restrictions on Transfer of the Lock-Up Shares provided herein, the Company,
without prejudice to any other right or remedy that the Company might have
under the Purchaser Agreement, in law or equity, shall be entitled to receive
the total amount of proceeds that such breaching Shareholder received from the
Transfer Notwithstanding anything to the contrary set forth above, no Transfer
of the Lock-up Shares shall be permitted hereunder until the purchase price
adjustment set forth in section 2(d) of the Purchase Agreement shall have
occurred.

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stock Transfer Instructions</B>. In furtherance of the restrictions
imposed under this Agreement, the Shareholders hereby authorize the Company and
its transfer agent to decline to make any transfer of Lock-up Shares if such
transfer would constitute a violation or breach of this Agreement. The
Shareholders also agree and consent to the entry of stock transfer instructions
with the Company&#146;s transfer agent against the transfer of any Lock-up Shares.

<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stock Legend</B>. The Shareholders agree that all certificates
representing all Lock-Up Shares will have endorsed upon them in bold-face type
a legend in substantially the following form:

<P align="center"><FONT size="2"><B>REGISTRATION RIGHTS AGREEMENT</B>
</FONT>




<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THIS REGISTRATION RIGHTS AGREEMENT (the &#147;Agreement&#148;) is made and entered
into effective as of the 30th day of January, 2003, by and among 1-800
CONTACTS, INC., a Delaware corporation (the &#147;Company&#148;), and CAMELOT
VENTURES/CJ, L.L.C. d/b/a LENS 1st, a Michigan limited liability company, and
LENS EXPRESS LLC, a Michigan limited liability company (collectively the
&#147;Investors&#148;).

</FONT>


<P align="center"><FONT size="2"><B>RECITALS</B>
</FONT>




<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company and the Investors have entered into an Asset Purchase
Agreement of even date herewith (the &#147;Purchase Agreement&#148;), whereby the Company
will purchase certain of the assets and assume certain of the liabilities of
the Investors.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;B.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pursuant to the Purchase Agreement, the purchase price for the Acquired
Assets will be paid by the Company by the delivery to the Investors of (1)
cash, (2)&nbsp;the Closing Buyer Shares, and (3)&nbsp;by the assumption by the Company of
the Assumed Liabilities. Pursuant to the Purchase Agreement, the Company has
agreed to grant to each of the Investors certain registration rights, to enable
the Investors to participate, under certain specified circumstances, in
registrations of the Common Stock of the Company with the Securities and
Exchange Commission, pursuant to the Securities Act of 1933, as amended.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The purpose of this Agreement is to document and establish the terms of
the registration rights granted to each of the Investors.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;D.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Terms not otherwise defined in this Agreement shall have the meanings
ascribed to them in the Purchase Agreement.

</FONT>


<P align="center"><FONT size="2"><B>AGREEMENT</B>
</FONT>




<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;NOW, THEREFORE, the parties hereto agree as follows:

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Definitions.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Act&#148; shall mean the Securities Act of 1933, as amended.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Closing Buyer Shares&#148; shall mean nine hundred thousand (900,000) shares
of the Company&#146;s Common Stock.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Commission&#148; shall mean the Securities and Exchange Commission.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Common Stock&#148; shall mean the Company&#146;s $0.01 par value common stock.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Exchange Act&#148; shall mean the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, as amended.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Holder&#148; or &#147;Holders&#148; shall mean any Investor or Investors holding
Registrable Securities (including shares of Common Stock convertible into
Registrable Securities) and any

</FONT>


<P align="center"><FONT size="2">&nbsp;</FONT>

<!-- PAGEBREAK -->
<P><HR noshade><P>
<H5 align="left" style="page-break-before:always">&nbsp;</H5><P>
<P><FONT size="2">person holding Registrable Securities to whom
the rights under this Agreement have been transferred, in accordance with
Section&nbsp;9 hereof.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The terms &#147;register,&#148; &#147;registered,&#148; and &#147;registration&#148; refer to a
registration effected by preparing and filing a registration statement in
compliance with the requirements of the Act and the declaration or ordering of
the effectiveness of such registration statement.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Piggyback Registration&#148; shall have the meaning set forth in Section&nbsp;2(a)
hereof.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Registrable Securities&#148; shall mean the Closing Buyer Shares and any
shares of Common Stock issued in respect of the Closing Buyer Shares, as a
result of a stock split, stock dividend, recapitalization or otherwise;
provided, however, that shares of Common Stock shall only be treated as
Registrable Securities if and so long as they have not been (i)&nbsp;sold to or
through a broker, dealer or underwriter in a public distribution or a public
securities transaction, or (ii)&nbsp;sold or all of the otherwise Registrable
Securities are available for sale in the opinion of counsel to the Company in a
transaction exempt from the registration and prospectus delivery requirements
of the Act so that all transfer restrictions and restrictive legends with
respect thereto are removed upon the consummation of the sale.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Registration Expenses&#148; shall mean all expenses incurred in complying with
Sections&nbsp;2 and 4 hereof, including all registration and filing fees, fees and
disbursements of counsel for the Company, blue sky fees and expenses, and the
reasonable fees and disbursements of one counsel for all Holders of Registrable
Securities participating in the registration, but excluding Selling Expenses.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Selling Expenses&#148; shall mean all underwriting discounts, selling
commissions and stock transfer taxes applicable to any securities registered or
sold by the Holders and all fees and disbursements of counsel or consultants
for the Holders (except for fees and disbursements of counsel included within
the definition of Registration Expenses).

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grant of Piggyback Registration Rights.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. Right to Piggyback. Subject to Section&nbsp;2(e) below, whenever the
Company proposes to register any of its securities under the Act in connection
with a public offering of such securities solely for cash (but not including
any registration relating solely to employee benefit plans or a registration
relating solely to a Commission Rule&nbsp;145 transaction), and the registration
form to be used may be used for the registration of Registrable Securities (a
&#147;Piggyback Registration&#148;), the Company shall give prompt written notice (before
the initial filing of the registration statement relating to such public
offering and at least twenty (20)&nbsp;days before the effective date of such
registration statement) to all Holders of Registrable Securities of its
intention to effect such a registration and shall use its best efforts, in
accordance with the terms and conditions of this Agreement, to include in such
registration all Registrable Securities with respect to which the Company has
received written requests for inclusion therein before the effective date of
the related registration statement. In the event that any Piggyback
Registration shall be an underwritten offering of securities of the Company,
any requests by the Holders pursuant to this Section&nbsp;2(a) to register
Registrable Securities must specify that such shares are to

</FONT>


<P align="center"><FONT size="2">2</FONT>

<!-- PAGEBREAK -->
<P><HR noshade><P>
<H5 align="left" style="page-break-before:always">&nbsp;</H5><P>
<P><FONT size="2">be included in the
underwriting on the same terms as the shares of Common Stock otherwise being
sold through such registration.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. Expenses Relating to Piggyback Registrations. The Registration
Expenses incidental to the Company&#146;s performance of, or compliance with, this
Agreement in connection with each Piggyback Registration shall be paid by the
Company. Except for the reasonable fees and disbursements of one counsel for
the Holders included in the Registration Expenses, each participating Holder
shall bear the fees and costs of its own counsel. All Selling Expenses
relating to the Registrable Securities being sold by a Holder in a Piggyback
Registration shall be paid by such selling Holder.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c. Priority on Primary Registrations. If a Piggyback Registration is an
underwritten primary registration on behalf of the Company, and the managing
underwriters advise the Company in writing that, in their opinion, the number
of securities requested to be included in such registration exceeds the number
which can be sold in an orderly manner in such offering within a price range
acceptable to the Company, the Company shall be entitled to cut-back the number
of securities to be included in such registration. In such event, securities
shall be included in the Piggyback Registration in accordance with the
following priority: (i)&nbsp;first, the securities the Company proposes to sell;
(ii)&nbsp;second, any securities proposed to be sold by shareholders of the Company
exercising any existing demand registration rights; and (iii)&nbsp;third, all
shareholders of the Company exercising piggyback registration rights, including
the Holders requesting that Registrable Securities be included in the
registration pursuant to the terms of this Agreement. In the event of a
cut-back of the number of securities that may be included in a Piggyback
Registration, the number of Registrable Securities and other securities of the
Company that may be included in the registration upon the exercise of piggyback
registration rights shall be allocated among all of the Company&#146;s shareholders
(including the Holders) who are exercising such Piggyback Registration rights,
in proportion, as nearly as practicable, to the respective amounts of
Registrable Securities and other securities which each such Holder requests to
be included in such registration.

</FONT>


<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;d. Priority on Secondary Registrations. If a Piggyback Registration is an
underwritten secondary registration of the Company&#146;s securities, and the
managing underwriters advise the Company in writing that, in their opinion, the
number of securities requested to be included in such registration exceeds the
number which can be sold in an orderly manner in such offering within a price
range acceptable to the Company and the Holders initially requesting such
registration, the Company shall be entitled to cut-back the number of
securities in such registration and include in such registration (i)&nbsp;first, any
securities proposed to be sold by any shareholders of the Company exercising
any existing demand registration rights; and (ii)&nbsp;second, the Registrable
Securities and other securities of the Company requested to be included therein
by the Holders and other shareholders of the Company exercising Piggyback
Registration rights. The number of Registrable Securities and other securities
of the Company that may be included in the registration on behalf of the
Holders and other shareholders exercising Piggyback Registration rights shall
be allocated among all of such Holders and other shareholders who are
exercising Piggyback Registration rights, in proportion, as nearly as
practicable, to the respective amounts of Registrable Securities and other
securities which each such Holder requests to be included in such registration.

</FONT>

<P><HR noshade><P>
<H5 align="left" style="page-break-before:always">&nbsp;</H5><P>
<P><FONT size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;e. Exception. Notwithstanding any of the foregoing provisions of this
Section&nbsp;2, the Holders of Registrable Securities shall not be entitled to
participate in, and the Company shall have no obligation to register any
